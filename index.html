Chauffeur service and priority, in detail

Official page: Virgin Atlantic chauffeur service

The chauffeur transfer

  • What it is: a private car (Mercedes E-Class, or an Estate for more luggage) from home to the airport before you fly out, and home again when you land back.
  • Complimentary only on the higher Upper Class booking classes (fare classes J, D, C and I). On a discounted Upper Class fare, or an Upper Class reward booking made with Flying Club miles, it is not automatically included. This is the key thing to confirm with Melissa for this package fare, as holiday-package Upper Class is sometimes a lower booking class.
  • Distance: the complimentary mileage varies by location. Beyond the included radius you can put Flying Club miles towards a car within 75 driven miles of the airport; in the US, beyond 75 miles is chargeable at about $3.50 per mile.
  • London alternatives: instead of the car you can take Heathrow Express, Gatwick Express, or a limo-bike.
  • Las Vegas end: the chauffeur network does cover Las Vegas, useful for the airport to Strip run.
  • Booking: arranged through the Upper Class team on +44 (0)344 412 4666 once your tickets are issued.

Priority, end to end

  • Heathrow Upper Class Wing: a private drive-in entrance with its own fast-track security, so you skip the main queues.
  • Priority check-in at a dedicated desk, then straight into the Clubhouse.
  • Priority boarding whenever you choose to board.
  • Priority baggage: bags are tagged priority and come off the carousel first.

Watching the race without tickets

The cars are seriously loud and the circuit runs right along the Strip, so you will hear the racing almost constantly from the hotel and as you walk around, and the atmosphere is everywhere. You will also catch fleeting glimpses of cars through gaps and from height. The easy free trackside views are deliberately fenced and the pedestrian bridges sheeted over, so for an actual watch you want a screen or a bit of elevation, but the noise and buzz come for free.

Spots to roam between (drinks, screens, height)

  • The Chandelier, The Cosmopolitan — multi-level crystal bar, lively and central on the circuit (and your hotel in Option B).
  • Eiffel Tower bar and restaurant, Paris Las Vegas — directly across from Bellagio with elevation and floor-to-ceiling Strip views, good for catching cars from height.
  • Vista Cocktail Lounge, Caesars Palace — Strip-facing lounge a short walk away.
  • Bellagio's own Strip-facing bars and the fountain terrace — you are staying on the circuit, so the easiest glimpses are on your doorstep, and Bellagio sells premium viewing of its section if you want a guaranteed look.
  • Any sportsbook or sports bar — the race is on big screens everywhere, free to wander into with a drink.

Height helps for real glimpses, so high floors, casino upper levels and any open walkway get you more. Expect crowds and Strip road closures on the race evenings.
